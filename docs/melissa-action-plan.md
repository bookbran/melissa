### 2. Validation Interview Guide

**Before the Interview:**
- Review their business briefly (website, LinkedIn)
- Have your pitch ready to share when asked
- Prepare to LISTEN more than talk (80/20 rule)

**Opening (2 min):**
> "Thank you so much for taking the time. I'm exploring a new direction and really value your perspective. I'll share a bit about what I'm thinking and then I'd love to hear your honest reactions â€” including what doesn't land. Sound good?"

**Your Pitch (2 min):**
> "I'm building a strategic partnership service for established entrepreneurs. Here's the idea: I help you separate what's actually moving your business forward from everything else competing for your attention. Then I either handle the other stuff, systematize it, or get it off your plate â€” so you can execute on what matters without everything else falling through the cracks. I'm not just a coach who asks questions and leaves you to execute. I'm not a VA waiting for task lists. I'm a partner who thinks with you AND stays in the work with you."

**Core Questions:**

**Understanding Their World:**
1. "What does a typical week look like for you right now â€” where does your time actually go?"
2. "What's the stuff you wish you could get off your plate but hasn't happened yet?"
3. "When you think about growing your business, what's the biggest thing getting in the way?"

**Testing the Offer:**
4. "When you hear what I described, what comes up for you? What's your gut reaction?"
5. "Does this feel like something you'd want? What would need to be true for it to be a 'hell yes'?"
6. "What would make you hesitate?"

**Language Discovery:**
7. "How would you describe the feeling when you're stuck doing stuff that's not really your thing?"
8. "What would it feel like to have that handled â€” what's the relief you're looking for?"

**Pricing Discovery:**
9. "If something like this existed and it was the right fit, what would feel like a reasonable investment â€” monthly or per project?"
10. "What would feel too cheap (and make you suspicious)? What would feel too expensive?"

**Ideal Client Discovery:**
11. "Who else do you know that might struggle with this same thing?"
12. "What kind of entrepreneur do you think would be the BEST fit for something like this?"

**Closing:**
> "This has been incredibly helpful. Is there anything else you'd want me to know? And is it okay if I follow up down the road as this takes shape?"

### 4. Handling "No" or Hesitation

**If someone declines the interview:**
> "Totally understand â€” thanks for letting me know! If you ever want to connect down the road, I'd love that."

(That's it. Move on. It's not about you.)

**If someone seems hesitant in the interview:**
> "I appreciate your honesty â€” that's exactly what I need to hear. Can you tell me more about what's giving you pause?"

**If someone says "this isn't for me":**
> "That's really helpful to know. Can I ask â€” who DO you think this would be perfect for?"
